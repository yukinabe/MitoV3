# Mito — Privacy Policy

_Last updated: [DATE]_

Mito ("we", "us") is a study app that turns flashcard review and focus sessions
into a pixel-art RPG. This policy explains what we collect, why, and the choices
you have. We try to collect as little as possible.

## Who this is for

Mito is intended for students aged 13 and older. It is not directed at children
under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you
believe a child under 13 has created an account, contact us and we will delete
it.

## What we collect

**Account information.** If you create an account, we store your **email address**
and a password (passwords are handled by our authentication provider and are
never stored by us in readable form). You can also use the app anonymously
without an account; in that case we create a random anonymous ID so your
progress can sync, with no email attached.

**Your study content.** The **flashcards and decks you create or import** (their
text), and your spaced-repetition review history, so your progress is saved and
can sync across your devices.

**Gameplay and usage data.** In-game progress (currency, team, streaks, daily
quests) and basic usage events (for example, opening the app, starting a study
session, finishing a battle) so the app works and so we can understand which
features are used and improve them.

**Social data (only if you use these features).** If you add friends, join or
create a class, or play co-op/versus, we store the membership and the decks you
choose to share into a class, plus a display name.

We do **not** collect your contacts, location, photos, or device advertising
identifiers, and we do **not** use your data for advertising or sell it.

## How we use your data

- To provide the core app: save and sync your decks, scheduling, and progress.
- To run social features you opt into (friends, classes, leaderboards, co-op).
- To grade typed answers in "Quiz" mode using an AI service (see below).
- To understand usage and improve the app.

We do **not** track you across other apps or websites.

## Third parties we share data with

We use a small number of service providers strictly to operate the app:

- **Supabase** (database, authentication, and storage) hosts your account,
  decks, and progress on our behalf.
- **DeepSeek** (AI grading) — when you use "Quiz" mode, the question, the correct
  answer, and your typed answer are sent to grade your response. This text is
  used only to return a grade and is not used to identify you. If you never use
  Quiz mode, nothing is sent.

These providers process data on our instructions; we don't permit them to use it
for their own purposes.

## Data retention and deletion

Your data is kept while your account exists. You can **delete your account at any
time** from **Settings → Account → Delete Account**. Deleting removes your
server-side account and associated data (decks, cards, progress, social
memberships), and clears Mito's data stored on your device. Anonymous data tied
to an unused anonymous ID is removed as part of normal cleanup.

You can also delete individual decks and cards at any time within the app.

## Your choices

- Use the app anonymously, with no email.
- Decline notifications (Mito only sends due-card and streak reminders if you
  allow them; you can turn them off in iOS Settings).
- Delete your account or individual content at any time.

## Security

Account sessions are stored in the device Keychain. Data in transit is encrypted
(HTTPS). No method of storage or transmission is 100% secure, but we take
reasonable measures to protect your information.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected
by the "Last updated" date above.
