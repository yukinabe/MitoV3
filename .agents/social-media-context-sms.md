# Social Media Context — Mito

> Voice foundation for all social-media-skills (carousel/hook/caption/post writers reference this).
> Goal: never sound like an ad. Sound like a real student who happens to be building a thing.

## Product
**Mito** — a study app where reviewing your real flashcards happens inside a cute pixel turn-based RPG. Focus/study time powers a squad of biology-organelle characters (Mito the mitochondria, Chloro, Astro, Neuro, Dendri, B Cell). Tagline lane: "a study RPG for the flashcards you already have." iPhone-first. Waitlist stage.

## Who it's for
College + late-high-school STEM students who:
- know active recall / flashcards work but can't make themselves open Anki
- have "gamer brain" — will grind a mobile game for hours but not study for 10 min
- are cooked during finals (quarter system especially)
- have a short attention span and are tired of being told to "just be disciplined"

## Voice (how the founder actually talks)
- **First person, casual, lowercase.** Like texting a smart friend, not addressing an audience.
- **Specific > clever.** Real numbers and real moments ("400 cards, finals in 3 days, opened anki zero times") beat punchlines.
- **Dry and self-deprecating.** The founder is one of the target users. Confess the bad habit, don't preach.
- **Build-in-public honesty.** "i'm building this because i needed it" — not "introducing the future of studying."
- **Calm confidence.** State the insight plainly. No hype words.

## Hard NOs (the stuff that screams "AI / marketer")
- ❌ Forced slang stacking: "your brain said no thoughts", "it's giving", "rizz", "gyat"
- ❌ Emoji as punctuation / personality crutch: 💀 🧍 🫡 🔥 sprinkled to seem young. (One natural emoji, rarely, is fine.)
- ❌ "POV:" unless it's literally a POV
- ❌ Parallel-structure ad formulas: "X: this. Y: that." used as a gimmick
- ❌ Hype/corporate: "revolutionize", "game-changer", "level up your studying", "unlock your potential"
- ❌ Over-explaining the product on the hook. Hide Mito until the viewer already cares (≈ slide 3+).
- ❌ Em-dashes everywhere, perfectly balanced sentences, tidy rule-of-three. Real people are messier.

## Yes (the stuff that reads human)
- ✅ A real confession or specific observation as the hook
- ✅ Lowercase, fragments, the occasional "ngl" / "honestly" / "same" used naturally
- ✅ One concrete detail that only a real student would say
- ✅ Lead with the pain or the gamer-brain identity; reveal Mito as "so i'm building…"
- ✅ Native CTA: "comment mito and i'll send it" / "waitlist's in my bio"

## Pillars (from marketing/content-bank.md)
1. Engineering/STEM study workflow (credibility)
2. Anti-boring flashcards (enemy = boredom, not Anki)
3. Gamer brain studying (identity)
4. Finals week survival (timely)

## Platforms
TikTok (photo carousel + video) and Instagram (carousel). 1080×1080 or 4:5/9:16. On-image text IS the hook. Low-polish wins. Caption sets up the swipe + CTA; don't duplicate the slides.

## CTA
Waitlist. "comment mito" → DM the link, or "link in bio." Track: saves, comments asking for the link, profile visits, signups.
