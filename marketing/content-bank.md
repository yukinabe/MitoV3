# Mito Content Bank

## Operating Rule

Mito content should not open like an ad. Lead with a student pain, study workflow, gamer identity, or useful list. Reveal Mito only after the viewer already cares.

## Weekly Production Loop

- Capture: dump every rough hook here immediately.
- Batch: pick 10 hooks twice a week and turn each into a slideshow outline.
- Produce: make simple square carousels first, then reuse the same ideas as UGC videos.
- Post: ship 2-4 pieces per day across TikTok and Instagram.
- Track: after 24 hours, mark each post as kill, tweak, or double down.

## Experiment Batch 001

Research notes:

- Slide 1 carries most of the post. Test hook psychology more than design polish.
- Use short carousels too. Some posts should be 2-5 slides, not always 9-10.
- Hide Mito until the viewer understands the study pain or workflow.
- Test different native formats: listicle, confession, warning, meme, workflow, anti-tool, identity callout.
- Keep product screenshots consistent; random mixed visual styles kill trust.

### Personas To Test

- The cracked STEM student: wants a serious workflow and trusts GPA/process content.
- The Anki avoider: knows flashcards work but hates opening review apps.
- The gamer brain student: likes RPGs, gacha, TCGs, collecting, turn-based games.
- The cooked finals student: wants help right now and responds to panic/humor.
- The low attention span student: wants permission to make studying less painful.
- The app-stack browser: likes "tools I use" and "top apps" posts.
- The brainrot scroller: reacts to blunt, chaotic, meme-native phrasing.

## Metrics To Track Manually

Track after 2 hours and 24 hours:

- Views
- Likes
- Saves
- Shares
- Profile visits
- Waitlist clicks/signups
- Comments asking for the link

Decision rule:

- High saves, low comments: make more useful workflow/list content.
- High comments, low saves: make more identity/hot-take content.
- High profile visits, low signups: tighten bio and pinned comment CTA.
- High views, low engagement: hook worked but slides/caption did not.
- Low views, high engagement: repost the same idea with a stronger first slide.
